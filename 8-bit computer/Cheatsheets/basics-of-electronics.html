Electronics Fundamentals

// Quick Reference — Voltage · Current · Resistance · Power

Quantity 01
V
Voltage
Unit: Volts (V)
The electrical pressure that drives current around a circuit. Also called electromotive force (EMF) or potential difference.
Water analogy: the pressure in the pipe — how hard the water is being pushed.
Quantity 02
I
Current
Unit: Amperes (A) · milliamperes (mA)
The rate of flow of electrical charge around a circuit. 1 mA = 0.001 A. LEDs typically run at 5–20 mA.
Water analogy: how many litres flow past a point per second.
Quantity 03
R
Resistance
Unit: Ohms (Ω) · kilohms (kΩ)
Opposition to the flow of current. Higher resistance means less current for a given voltage. 1 kΩ = 1,000 Ω.
Water analogy: the narrowness of the pipe — a tighter pipe restricts flow.
Quantity 04
P
Power
Unit: Watts (W) · milliwatts (mW)
The rate at which energy is consumed or converted. Resistors dissipate power as heat — stay within their watt rating.
Water analogy: the rate at which the flowing water turns a waterwheel — how fast work is being done.
Ohm's Law — Cover the unknown to find the formula
V = I × R
Cover V → I and R side by side = multiply
I = V ÷ R
Cover I → V above R = divide
R = V ÷ I
Cover R → V above I = divide
Power Triangle
P = I × V
I = P ÷ V
V = P ÷ I
Energy vs Power
Power is the rate of energy use. Energy is the total amount consumed over time.
Energy = P × Time
Energy unit: Joules (J) in physics
On your electricity bill: kilowatt-hours (kWh)
Worked Example — LED Current Limiting Resistor
Supply: 5 V  ·  Red LED forward voltage (VLED): 2 V  ·  Target current (ILED): 10 mA
STEP 01
V_R = 5 − 2 = 3 V
Subtract the LED's forward voltage from supply. The resistor must drop this remainder.
STEP 02
R = 3 ÷ 0.01 = 300 Ω
Ohm's Law: R = V ÷ I. Use 0.01 A (= 10 mA). Round up to nearest standard value.
STEP 03
→ Use 330 Ω
Nearest standard value above 300 Ω. Ben Eater uses this throughout his build.
STEP 04 — CHECK
P = 3 × 0.01 = 0.03 W
Power in resistor = V_R × I. Well under ¼ W rating of a standard resistor. Safe.
LED Forward Voltages (VLED)
Red 1.8 – 2.2 V
Yellow 2.0 – 2.2 V
Green 2.0 – 3.5 V
Blue 3.0 – 3.5 V
White 3.0 – 3.5 V
Always check the datasheet for your specific LED. If unavailable: assume 2 V for red/yellow, 3.2 V for blue/white.
The Water Pipe Analogy — at a glance
💧
Voltage (V)
Water pressure pushing through the pipe
🌊
Current (I)
Flow rate — litres passing a point per second
🔧
Resistance (R)
Narrowness of the pipe — restricts flow
⚙️
Power (P)
Rate the water turns a waterwheel — work done per second
Key rule: Higher pressure (V) through the same pipe (R) = more flow (I). The resistor narrows the pipe to keep current within safe limits.
